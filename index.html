About Us

The Irish Guards are one of the Foot Guards regiments of the British Army, founded in 1900 by Queen Victoria in recognition of the Irish soldiers' service in the Second Boer War. Their motto, Quis Separabit ("Who Shall Separate Us"), reflects the regiment’s unity and loyalty.

The regiment performs both ceremonial duties, including the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace, and operational roles, having served in major conflicts including World Wars, Northern Ireland, Iraq, and Afghanistan. The Irish Guards are known for their distinctive dark blue tunics, red plume in the headdress, and exceptional precision in drill.

Members of the regiment take pride in their heritage, discipline, and esprit de corps, upholding traditions while adapting to modern military challenges. Joining the Irish Guards is both an honor and a commitment to serve the Crown and country with distinction.
